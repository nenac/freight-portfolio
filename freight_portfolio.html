Why this matters

Hyperscale is building the AI-native intelligence layer for physical operations, starting with automating high-volume carrier workflows. This project shows how I approach the same class of problem: I identified the highest-friction operational bottleneck, mapped the end-to-end process across five tools and three teams, and built a structured system that turned fragmented manual work into a repeatable, trackable workflow. The same systems thinking that automated freight coordination for a B2B manufacturer applies directly to building the operational backbone for a fast-scaling AI company.

The Challenge

Freight coordination was the company's biggest operational bottleneck

As a B2B manufacturer shipping heavy, oversized equipment to pharmaceutical, beverage, and food processing customers nationwide, freight logistics touched every department. But the process was entirely manual, with no standardized workflow, no centralized tracking, and no visibility into shipment status.

Before: Manual and fragmented

  • Customer shipping details collected via ad hoc emails and phone calls with no standard format
  • Freight quotes scattered across individual email threads with no comparison framework
  • No centralized tracking from quote request through delivery confirmation
  • Packing dimensions, weights, and values re-entered multiple times across systems
  • Team members relied on memory and sticky notes for shipment status updates
  • Customers called repeatedly asking for tracking numbers and delivery estimates

After: Automated and trackable

  • Standardized customer survey captures all shipping details in one submission
  • Quotes auto-populate a calculation sheet for instant three-way comparison
  • Single workbook tracks every shipment from intake through delivery with status columns
  • Data enters once at intake and flows through all downstream steps automatically
  • Physical whiteboard and digital tracking stay synchronized across team
  • Templated emails deliver quotes, invoices, and tracking proactively to customers
System Architecture

Five connected tools, one unified workflow

Rather than purchasing new software, I built this system by connecting existing tools the team already used. The Google Sheets workbook serves as the operational hub, with four purpose-built tabs that move data through the pipeline without re-entry.

Design Rationale

Why I built it this way

Every design choice prioritized adoption speed, cost efficiency, and reliability for a small team operating in a fast-paced manufacturing environment.

Cost Efficiency

Google Sheets as the operational hub

The team already used Google Workspace daily. Building on existing tools meant zero licensing costs, no training overhead, and immediate adoption. The four-tab architecture separates concerns while keeping everything accessible in one workbook.

Data Integrity

Structured form intake replaces free-text emails

The Google Form enforces required fields and consistent formatting. This eliminated the most common delays: missing dock hours, incomplete addresses, and unspecified freight class requirements.

Speed

Email templates in Front for every touchpoint

Four pre-built templates (survey link, broker request, customer quotes, tracking confirmation) reduced per-shipment communication time and ensured consistent, professional messaging at every step.

Scalability

Separated tabs for each workflow stage

Form Responses captures raw input. In Motion Tracking manages active shipments. Freight Calculations handles pricing. Invoice Tracking closes the loop. This separation means any single tab can be upgraded or automated independently without breaking the whole system.

Accountability

Payment gate before fulfillment

The hard stop between payment collection (step 8) and dispatch (step 9) prevents the most expensive operational error: shipping equipment before payment is secured. This single rule change eliminated revenue leakage.

Customer Experience

Proactive tracking communication

By building the tracking notification into the workflow as a required final step, customers receive their invoice and PRO number without asking. This reduced inbound status inquiries and improved the post-sale experience.
